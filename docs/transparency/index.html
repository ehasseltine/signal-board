Signal Board is built on the belief that people deserve to understand not just what the news says, but how the systems delivering that news actually operate. That commitment starts with being fully transparent about how this tool itself operates.

What Signal Board does

Every day, Signal Board reads publicly available RSS feeds from hundreds of news outlets, podcasts, newsletters, government agencies, and research institutions. It identifies what stories are being covered, which sources are covering them, where the coverage overlaps across different perspectives and communities, and where it diverges. The analysis you see on this site is produced from that daily reading.

The goal is not to replace any of these sources or to tell you what to think about what they publish. The goal is to make visible the patterns and connections that no single outlet shows you on its own, because no single outlet reads everything.

What happens to source material

This is the part that matters most for copyright and ethical use, so here it is plainly.

RSS feeds are read

Signal Board reads RSS feeds, which are public data formats that news organizations publish specifically so that machines can read them. This is the same technology that powers podcast apps, news readers, and services like Google News and Apple News.

Article titles and metadata are extracted

From each feed entry, the tool extracts the article title, the source name, the publication date, and the URL. Article titles are short, factual identifiers and are not subject to copyright protection.

Content is classified, not reproduced

When fuller text is available in a feed (as it is with many newsletters and podcasts), that text is used internally to classify the article's subject matter more accurately. The text itself is never displayed to readers, never stored in the public output, and never reproduced on this site.

Original analysis is produced

The patterns, connections, and questions you see on Signal Board are original analysis generated from the aggregate data. No article summaries, no paraphrased passages, no quotes from source material appear in the output.

Every article links back to its source

When an article title appears on Signal Board, it links directly to the original publication. The intent is always to drive readers toward the original reporting, not away from it.

Ethical commitments

No original content is reproduced

Signal Board does not copy, paraphrase, summarize, or display the body text of any article from any source. The analysis on this site is entirely original work produced from the patterns in the data, not from the content of individual articles.

Sources are always attributed and linked

Every article that appears on this site includes the name of its source and a direct link to the original. The goal is to surface journalism and send readers to it, not to substitute for it.

Source selection is documented and justified

Every source tracked by Signal Board is listed on the Sources page with a description of what it is and why it's included. There are no hidden sources and no undisclosed criteria. If a source is here, you can see why.

The methodology is open

The How It Works page describes the technical process in plain language. The analysis pipeline uses AI classification to tag articles by subject area, and the clustering and connection logic is designed to surface substantive cross-source coverage rather than viral volume.

Inclusivity is structural, not performative

The source list is built to represent the actual information ecosystem as Americans experience it, across political perspectives, geographic regions, languages, demographics, and media formats. Community outlets, international press, podcasts, newsletters, and government primary sources sit alongside national newspapers because they all contribute to the picture.

AI is a tool, not an author

Signal Board uses AI for one specific task: classifying articles by subject area more accurately than keyword matching alone can do. The editorial judgments, the framework, the source selection, and the analytical perspective are human decisions. AI does not write the analysis, choose the sources, or determine what matters.

Legal basis

Signal Board's approach to source material follows established legal and industry practice. Reading publicly available RSS feeds and displaying article titles with links to original sources is the foundation of every news aggregation service, from Google News to Apple News to every RSS reader application. Article titles are factual identifiers that do not meet the threshold for copyright protection. No copyrighted content from any source is reproduced, displayed, or distributed through this site.

The analysis produced by Signal Board is original work. It examines patterns across sources in aggregate and produces its own synthesis, which is fundamentally different from reproducing or summarizing any individual source's reporting.
