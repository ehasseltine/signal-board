How we did this

Signal Board pulls articles from 275 RSS feeds every morning, covering U.S. newsrooms, international press, local and regional outlets, think tanks, specialist journals, and explainer sites. Each article is classified by an AI model (Claude Haiku) that identifies which topics it covers and how it connects to other stories. About 70% of articles receive a full analysis. The stories you see above are grouped by keyword clustering and then synthesized by AI into coherent paragraphs. A human editor reviews the output for accuracy, tone, and writing quality.

Source selection is editorial: we chose outlets that represent different perspectives, geographies, and communities, with a deliberate emphasis on sources that are underrepresented in algorithmic news feeds. The full source list is available on the Sources page.

This is a working prototype. It has blind spots, particularly in non-English language coverage and in communities without strong digital news infrastructure. If you notice something missing, that is useful information. Tell us.
