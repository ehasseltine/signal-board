About Signal Board

Built by Elise Hasseltine

The Thesis

The thing humans do that matters most is think well together, and the current information architecture is degrading that capacity. All the forces reshaping the world, whether AI, economic transformation, climate, democratic erosion, or global power shifts, are deeply interconnected. The information systems through which most people encounter them make it nearly impossible to see the connections. Signal Board exists to change that.

The Philosophy

Signal Board starts from a conviction that people are not inherently selfish, as we have been told to believe, but inherently good, and that they do their best to make the world a better place. The evidence for this is extensive, from evolutionary biology's concept of "survival of the friendliest" to over 700 field studies on disaster response showing that when catastrophe hits, the typical human response is spontaneous mutual aid, not panic. The information architecture we rely on is structurally incapable of conveying this, because decency does not sell ads and cooperation is not newsworthy under the current definition of what news is supposed to be.

Signal Board exists to illuminate the goodness amid the darkness. Not by ignoring the darkness, which is real, but by building an information architecture that can see the full picture.

The Method

Signal Board reads 275 news sources every day, from the New York Times to local newsrooms in Mississippi, from the Financial Times to community journalists in South Africa, and synthesizes them into connected mega-stories that show how the biggest forces shaping the world are linked.

Every source, every day, gets read through seven questions:

  • Who is thinking well together here, and who has been cut out?
  • What is the information architecture underneath this, and is it carrying knowledge to the people who need it?
  • Where does this connect to something I have read in a different domain?
  • What does this look like for someone living inside it?
  • What would the honest version of this conversation sound like?
  • If I could only tell someone one thing from this source, what would change how they see the world?
  • Where in this story are people being decent, and why is that not the headline?

The methodology is detailed on the How It Works page.

Who Built This

My name is Elise Hasseltine. I build Signal Board because I believe the work of understanding what is happening to us, and explaining it clearly so that other people can understand it too, is among the most important things a person can do right now.

I grew up in a home built around openness and connection, where curiosity about other people's experiences was how you actually began to understand the world. That conviction drives everything Signal Board does.

Signal Board is a learning project built in public. The framework will change as the source base grows, as the automation evolves, and as I learn things that complicate or deepen the argument. That is the point.

Transparency

Signal Board uses AI (Claude by Anthropic) to classify articles by domain and generate initial synthesis drafts. The AI classification is not perfect, and misclassification rates are still being measured and improved. All synthesis is reviewed and the methodology is described in detail on the How It Works page.

Source bias ratings are provided by AllSides (CC BY-NC 4.0) and supplemented with manual research. Signal Board is not affiliated with any political organization, media company, or think tank. It is funded entirely by me.
