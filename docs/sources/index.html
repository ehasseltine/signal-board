How we selected these sources

Signal Board's source list balances reach, expertise, and perspective. We include major news outlets because they set the mainstream narrative. We include regional outlets because crucial stories happen outside national headlines. We include independent journalists and community voices because they report on stories that mainstream media overlooks. And we include experts and analysts because good journalism needs context.

The goal is not to be comprehensive or neutral. The goal is to see clearly. We actively look for sources that offer viewpoints and coverage that complement each other. Someone reading Signal Board should come away with a richer understanding of what's happening in the world—not because we've picked the "right" outlets, but because we've picked outlets that talk to each other and challenge each other.

The eight tiers:

  • U.S. Newsrooms: Major newspapers and broadcast networks that set the American news agenda and reach millions of people.
  • Global Press: International news organizations that cover the world from outside the U.S. perspective, including regional leaders in Asia, Africa, Europe, and Latin America.
  • Community Voices: Regional newsrooms, outlets serving specific communities (LGBTQ+, immigrant, Black, rural, etc.), and local accountability journalism.
  • Deep Expertise: Specialist outlets focused on specific domains: technology, labor, climate, health, defense, investigative journalism, and more.
  • Explainers: Outlets that translate complex stories into language anyone can understand, without sacrificing accuracy.
  • Research and Analysis: Think tanks, research institutes, and journals publishing expert analysis on policy and global affairs.
  • Newsletters and Podcasts: Long-form audio and written analysis that goes deeper into a smaller number of stories.
  • Government Sources: Primary documents from Congress, the courts, and federal agencies—the record of what government is actually doing.

All sources are updated daily. Some are active only once a week or on major news breaks; others publish multiple times per day. Signal Board weighs all sources equally regardless of publication frequency, treating each as a voice in the conversation about what's happening.
